Still Life

María Blanchard

The image is composed of numerous overlapping colored geometric planes. This superposition gives the work a sensation of spatiality, in contrast to the two-dimensionality characteristic of Analytical Cubism as well as other canvases from the artist's same period. The monochrome and smaller planes of analytical cubism give way here to others of greater chromatic richness, which through their different combinations suggest concrete forms of reality. Starting in 1919, the objects with which she composed her still lifes began to be identified more easily. Thus begins the way back to 1935ration, although her cubist experience will leave a clearly visible mark on her later painting.

The work present in Oviedo, which is not dated or signed, is developed in a frequent vertical format in all his cubist canvases. As is usual in these compositions, in the lower plane a table arranged with one of its corners facing the viewer can be recognized, which contributes to creating a certain sense of depth. On it, the artist has a still life whose objects are difficult to identify after the deconstruction process to which they have been subjected. A vertical element stands out on the whole that perhaps can be identified with a bottle, due to similarities with other works of the same theme and style.
