Three women

Fernand Leger, France, 1921

Three women (and a cat) painted in a geometric and dislocated way, as a good cubist would do. These robot women have tea here in an art-deco apartment and stare at us strange humans.

The rounded volumes are an evolution of the pre-cubist Cézanne, a painter who changed the life of the young Léger. From this epiphany, the artist began to paint his mechanized themes and modern life from cylinders, cones and spheres, built with primary colors, and all presented in very harmonic compositions.
